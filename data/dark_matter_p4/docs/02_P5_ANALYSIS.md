# P_5 = 2.02 keV DM — Analysis Result
**2026-07-21 | P_n spectrum warm DM candidate**

## Key Numbers
- P_5 = 2.023×10⁻⁶ GeV = 2.02 keV
- Coupling g_P5 ≈ 5.8×10⁻²⁵
- Lifetime (ν+γ decay): ~2.6×10³⁴ yr ✅ (above X-ray bound)

## DM Viability

| Mechanism | Ωh² predicted | Ωh² required | Verdict |
|:----------|:-------------:|:------------:|:-------:|
| Thermal freeze-out | ~10⁻⁶⁰ | 0.12 | ❌ |
| Freeze-in (direct) | 3×10⁻¹³ | 0.12 | ❌ |
| P_4 decay production | 2×10⁻¹⁵ | 0.12 | ❌ |

**P_5 not viable as dominant DM.** Same problem as P_4 — the P_n loop coupling is too weak.

## X-ray Constraints
- P_5 → ν + γ gives line at E_γ ≈ m_P5/2 ≈ 1.01 keV
- τ_decay ≈ 2.6×10³⁴ yr >> 10²⁷ yr (XMM-Newton bound)
- Not ruled out, but also not detectable as a decay signal

## Lyman-α Bound
- Thermal WDM: m > 3.5 keV (conservative)
- P_5 = 2.02 keV: below bound
- Non-thermal production would relax this, but abundance is too low anyway

## Conclusion
P_n particles are not viable DM candidates — their loop-suppressed couplings make them effectively sterile. The true DM in IDCM remains open (topological defects? axion from SO(10)? boundary states?).
